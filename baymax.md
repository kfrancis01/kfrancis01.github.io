# Automated Diagnostic Pipeline
**2024 – 2025**

[← Back to Projects](/)

---

## Overview

Developed a data pipeline that translated operational robotic failure data into structured, actionable datasets for AI research teams — accelerating model retraining cycles and improving the quality of training signal available for ML diagnostic tools.

---

## Problem

ML diagnostic tools were producing outputs that contained gaps preventing research teams from efficiently retraining models. Raw operational failure data wasn't structured in a way that mapped to actionable training signal — the connection between what field engineers observed and what AI researchers needed for model improvement was broken.

---

## Solution

Built a pipeline that:
- Ingested raw operational failure data from robotic systems
- Classified failure modes using structured categorization
- Generated structured datasets with clear failure categories and recommended actions
- Provided AI research teams with directly usable training data

The pipeline served as a bridge between field observations and research needs — translating the unstructured reality of production failures into the structured datasets that ML models require for effective retraining.

---

## Impact

- Accelerated model retraining cycles for AI research teams
- Improved quality of training signal by structuring previously unstructured failure data
- Reduced the gap between field failure observations and research team response time
- Established a repeatable methodology for operational-to-research data translation
